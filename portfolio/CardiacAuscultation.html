CARDIAC AUSCULTATION PROTOTYPE

Description (Excerpt from final report)

Cardiac auscultation is the act of making medical diagnoses through listening to sounds within the body. There has been a notable lack of cardiac auscultation skill proficiency in new medical graduates. This lack of skill can result in unnecessary tests for patients with normal results and potentially missed diagnoses and treatment in patients with cardiac disease. A key barrier to skills acquisition is insufficient practice time to develop the core ability to differentiate normal from abnormal heart sounds. This paper introduces a serious game mobile application that is designed to utilize repetition and real heart sounds to improve skill competency in new healthcare professionals.


The basic objective of the prototype requires the player to distinguish between normal and abnormal heart sounds. The player listens to the heart sound and is first asked to answer the question “Is this healthy?”. If the player answers correctly, they are awarded a point. If the heart sound does not indicate a healthy heartbeat, the player is then prompted to determine what type of atypical heart sound is represented, such as a murmur. The application integrates both audio and video. The heart sounds are played, and there are sound effects that help reinforce the reward of getting the correct answer. As for the visual component, the heart image moves to represent the motion of the heart and complement the audio track. The presence of audio and visual feedback to integrate both sight and sound as well as the ability to incorporate timing references improved the learning value of the prototype.
